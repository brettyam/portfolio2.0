Tyemill Portfolio Compare Web App

UX & Web Development

  • Client: Tyemill
  • Date: June - Sept 2014 (10 weeks)
  • Role: UX Designer & Front End Developer

Tyemill is a small but established company located in Queen Anne of Seattle. Interning there was my first opportunity to leverage what I have learned in school in a real environment. I acted as the main designer among four other Ruby on Rails developers with the goal of realizing business portfolios, creating them, and comparing their performances against other portfolios.

San Francisco Yellow Cab
Mobile App

Mobile UX

  • Client: N/A (Academic Project)
  • Date: Jan - Mar 2014 (10 weeks)
  • Role: UX Designer

This project was assigned in an information architecture course. We worked in groups of two with the goal of creating a complete user experience in which a user would like to schedule a taxi ride from point A to B but would like to share the ride with other individuals with a similar path in order to split the cost. Doing so required a strong understanding of the problem space, the user scenarios, creating respective flow charts, and taking account for unexpected edge cases. We also got some exposure to designing with branding strategies in mind.

The end result of the project was an interactive prototype in Axure that reflected the user flow we had arrived at based on our research. Aesthetically it is not meant to represent what we could expect a final version to look like, but it is what we could expect the interaction to feel like. However, above are a couple of higher fidelity mockups that we built towards the end of the project.

Tess Rental Website

Web Development

  • Client: Tess Ramos
  • Date: Nov - Dec 2013 (3 weeks)
  • Role: Javascript Developer

In this project I worked with a group of four, and it was the first exposure for all of us in working with a real client. The project was to create a website for a local housing agency that could show the different available units and their availability. I acted as the main developer, being mostly accountable for the Javascript components, though among a team of talented designers and a PM.

The website utilizes jQuery and is very basic - my Javascript capabilities have since grown. It is mainly responsible for rendering the necessary HTML for each "Neighborhood" page so as to reduce redundancy and avoid hard coding the HTML files themselves. Though my role was not as a designer, I was able to practice my abilities on both fronts as I was still able to include my design inputs around received stakeholder feedback.

Encaption Mobile App

Mobile UX & Research

  • Client: Laura Ackerman
  • Date: Sept - Dec 2013 (10 weeks)
  • Role: UX Designer & Project Manager

This is one of my more favorite projects. It was unique in that we worked with a disabled (half blind half deaf) user who would be our SME in designing a voice to text translator application. This required us to delve into much user research including interviews and usability testing with her every two weeks in which the design was being challeneged by the user, not the user being challenged by the design. Deliverables also ranged from creating paper prototypes, a higher fidelity prototype in Balsamiq, and a design specification which can be found by viewing the full project. The end of the project essentially mimics the design spec being passed on to a team of developers for building. Disclaimer: it is quite extensive!

The project was also very humbling to be a part of. Not only did our SME improve our design, but she also reminded us of how lucky we are as human beings to have full capability of our senses that we often take for granted. A more personalized reflection of the process can also be found here.

Bubble Mobile OS

Mobile UX & Design

  • Client: N/A (Academic Project)
  • Date: Apr - June 2014 (10 weeks)
  • Role: UX and Visual Designer

For this project, the class as a single team was assigned with the task of designing a brand new mobile operating system in just ten weeks. The idea the class decided on was "Bubble", an OS that placed its value on multitasking and speed without a centralized home screen.

For the first eight weeks we worked on developing a new, unique design language and branding strategies to make the overall experience feel as unified and consistent as possible. A nice styleguide was also created by fellow students that the class was meant to adhere to. The final two weeks required us to break off into subteams, responsible for designing the UX and respective UI's for simple use cases within expected first party OS apps. Our team was accountable for the Music Player application and designing the user experience around simply finding and playing a desired song.
